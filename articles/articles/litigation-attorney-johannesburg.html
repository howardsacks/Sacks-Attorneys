What to expect in a litigation matter

Litigation is a structured process. The strongest results usually come from early preparation: clear facts, the right documents, and a realistic view of costs, timelines and risks.

1) Confirm the dispute and the remedy

  • What exactly happened, and what outcome do you want (payment, interdict, damages, specific performance)?
  • What legal basis supports the claim or defence (contract, delict, statute)?
  • Is there urgency (interdict/urgent application) or can it run in the ordinary course?

2) Collect and preserve evidence

  • Contracts, emails/WhatsApp, invoices, delivery notes, bank proof, photographs, CCTV, call logs.
  • Names and contact details of witnesses.
  • Chronology: dates, events, and what was said/done.

3) Pre-litigation steps

  • Demand letters / notices (where required).
  • Settlement options (often cheaper and faster).
  • Jurisdiction and forum: Magistrates’ Court vs High Court, CCMA/Labour Court (where applicable).

4) Court steps (high level)

  • Issue and serve summons / application.
  • Pleadings / answering papers.
  • Discovery and preparation.
  • Hearings, trial, judgment, and enforcement (writs, attachments, etc.).

Timelines and costs

Timelines vary depending on urgency, the court roll, and the complexity of the evidence. We focus on keeping steps efficient and pushing the matter forward with clear updates.
